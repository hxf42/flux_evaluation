Introduction

With the rapid development of diffusion models, a multitude of text-to-image (T2I) models have emerged, such as Imagen, OpenAI's DALL·E 3, the Stable Diffusion series, and Lumina-T2I models, among others. Recently, Black Forest Lab released FLUX.1, and Ideogram 2.0 also made its debut, showcasing exceptional performance. In this article, we delve deeply into the capabilities and performance of FLUX, Ideogram 2.0, and other T2I models to address the following question: Have T2I models entered a new era, and can these breakthroughs lead T2I models toward becoming general-purpose models?
Today’s T2I model evaluations often rely on traditional metrics, such as using F1 scores to assess the realism of generated images, or calculating image-text similarity scores to judge the alignment between generated content and prompts. However, as more powerful models emerge, T2I models are no longer limited to traditional image generation tasks. They are beginning to demonstrate their potential in more challenging scenarios, gradually evolving toward the realm of foundation models. In this article, we introduce the T2I-Evaluation platform, where we thoroughly and comprehensively explore both open-source and closed-source T2I models. To evaluate these models scientifically and systematically, we have designed five domains to rigorously assess and compare their capabilities. These domains include Structured Output Generation, Realism and Physical Consistency Tasks, Specific Domain Generation, Challenging Scenario Generation, and Robotics and Simulation Tasks.
In this work, we carefully selected six models for comparison, including FLUX, ID2, MidJourney, DALL·E 3, SD3, and Jimeng. These models are all highly mature and widely recognized in the industry, with the recently launched FLUX and ID2 performing exceptionally well on many challenging tasks. These six models encompass both open-source and closed-source models, providing diversity and representativeness.
